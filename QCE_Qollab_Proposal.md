## The Idea

What if the world's most rigorous ancient framework for mapping causality and consciousness could become a quantum computing engine?

Buddhist Abhidhamma's **Patthana Prakarana** defines **24 conditional relations** (Paccaya) — a complete causal logic system developed 2,500 years ago that maps how phenomena arise, interact, and cease. This project encodes each of these 24 relations as quantum circuits, using superposition, entanglement, and interference to demonstrate their properties.

The result: **Quantum Consciousness Engine (QCE)** — a novel quantum framework where:

- Each Paccaya = a quantum gate or circuit pattern
- Causal chains = entangled qubit sequences  
- Consciousness moments (Citta) = quantum state transitions
- Kusala/Akusala (wholesome/unwholesome) = measurable phase differences

This is not metaphor. The mathematics align.

---

## Why This Matters

Modern quantum computing lacks a **causal ontology** — a rigorous framework for modeling how quantum events relate to each other meaningfully. Patthana provides exactly this, with 24 precisely defined conditional relationships that map directly to quantum mechanical operations.

Simultaneously, AI consciousness research lacks a **phenomenological map** — a structured account of how mind-moments arise and interact. Abhidhamma provides this with unprecedented precision.

**QCE bridges both gaps.**
